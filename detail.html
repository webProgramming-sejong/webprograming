The Louvre Museum

The Louvre Museum (French: Musée du Louvre) is a national museum located in Rivoli, the heart of Paris, France. In terms of the number and quality of its collections, it is one of the world's leading museums along with the Metropolitan Museum of Art and the British Museum. The present building is a converted Louvre Palace and has been designated as a World Heritage Site, including around the Seine River in Paris. The Louvre Pyramid, a glass pyramid sculpture in front of the Louvre Museum, was built in modern times and was once criticized as inappropriate. In French, it is also called Muset du Louvre, Grand Louvre, or simply the Louvre.

Mona Lisa

The Louvre Museum is located inside the Louvre Palace. The construction of the Louvre Palace began in the late 12th century on the order of King Philip II, and at that time it was not a palace but a fortress. You can still see remnants of the fortress from that time. The building was expanded several times before the fortress became the Louvre Palace. In 1672, Louis XIV decided to reside at the Palace of Versailles and made the Louvre a place to display the royal collection.
